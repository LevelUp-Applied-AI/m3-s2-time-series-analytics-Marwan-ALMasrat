# Executive Report: Revenue & Customer Analysis — FY 2025–2026

---

## Revenue Trends

Over the 12-month period from April 2025 to March 2026, total revenue grew
from $225K in April to $1.59M in March 2026 — representing sustained
top-line growth across the year.

Quarter-over-quarter growth was exceptional in Q3 2025 (+75.6%) and
Q4 2025 (+143.2%), driven primarily by the holiday season. December 2025
alone generated $1.79M, the highest single month on record, with daily
revenue peaking above $80K on December 22–23.

Q1 2026 declined -14.1% QoQ ($3.33M vs $3.88M in Q4 2025), which is
expected post-holiday. However, March 2026 recovered strongly with
$1.59M and +61.2% MoM growth, suggesting healthy demand returning.

The October spike (+34.2% MoM revenue, +60.2% MoM orders) is notable —
order volume grew nearly twice as fast as revenue that month, indicating
a higher share of lower-value orders, possibly driven by promotions or
category mix shift.

## Customer Retention

Cohort retention improved consistently throughout the year:

| Cohort     | Size | 30-Day % | 60-Day % | 90-Day % |
|------------|------|----------|----------|----------|
| April 2025 | 514  | 49.0%    | 71.0%    | 81.9%    |
| July 2025  | 388  | 36.3%    | 56.4%    | 74.0%    |
| October 2025 | 482 | 52.7%  | 78.6%    | 92.3%    |
| December 2025 | 611 | 65.1% | 84.3%    | 95.4%    |
| February 2026 | 460 | 90.4% | 98.3%    | 98.3%    |

The July 2025 cohort is the weakest performer, with only 36.3% returning
within 30 days. This may reflect summer acquisition channels bringing in
lower-intent customers.

The October–March cohorts show dramatically higher retention, with
February 2026 reaching 90.4% at 30 days. This likely reflects holiday
and post-holiday acquisition of high-intent buyers, or improved
onboarding and re-engagement campaigns during that period.

Note: February and March 2026 cohort 60/90-day numbers are artificially
high because the data window does not extend far enough — these customers
have not had a full 90-day window yet.

## Category Performance

Electronics dominates revenue share at ~50–60% throughout the year,
growing from $110K in April 2025 to $949K in March 2026 — an 8.6x
increase. Its share has been rising (49.1% in April → 59.7% in March),
meaning it is growing faster than every other category.

Home & Kitchen and Sports are the next largest categories, each holding
roughly 13–16% share consistently. Both grew in absolute terms but lost
slight share to Electronics.

Clothing declined in share from 13.7% in April 2025 to 9.6% in March
2026, despite growing in absolute revenue. This represents meaningful
mix shift away from Clothing.

Books and Health & Beauty remain small contributors (~1% and ~4%
respectively) with no meaningful share change.

The 3-month moving average confirms Electronics acceleration is not
seasonal noise — its smoothed trend line rose from $110K in April to
$949K in March with no reversal.

## Recommendations

1. **Double down on Q4 acquisition but improve July cohort quality.**
   July cohorts retain at only 36.3% at 30 days vs 65%+ for later
   cohorts. Audit the acquisition channels active in summer 2025 and
   reduce spend on those with low retention signals.

2. **Invest in Electronics inventory and margin management.**
   Electronics now represents nearly 60% of revenue and is still
   growing its share. Supply chain and pricing strategy for this
   category should be the top priority for Q2 2026 budget allocation.

3. **Investigate the October order volume spike.**
   Orders grew 60.2% MoM in October but revenue only grew 34.2%,
   meaning average order value dropped. Determine whether this was
   caused by discounting, product mix, or a specific campaign — and
   assess whether it was profitable.

4. **Protect and grow the Consumer segment.**
   Consumer accounts for the largest revenue segment ($6.88M cumulative)
   and showed +63% MoM growth in March 2026. Retention programs and
   loyalty incentives targeted at this segment will have the highest
   absolute revenue impact heading into Q2 2026.
